Background Checks


Washington allows ten business days from the date the licensed dealer submits a request for a background check to be completed, unless the purchaser clears the check before then, with some exceptions. An expected waiting period for a background check depends on type of background check to perform which depends on the firearm type.

Shotguns, Long Guns, and others serialized items are performed by the National Instant Criminal Background System (NICS), see links in "Resources" section to learn more i.e. operational reports and appeals. This Federal background, NICS's, since it's inception there is approximately less than a percent chance of denial . Pistol background checks are currently more of a manual process performed by local law enforcement officials, referred to as an enhanced background check, and includes additional WA WSP and HCA records, see WA ATG, WA DOL, and WA WSP for more information.

WA WSP is rolling out a new single point of contact system for all firearm types, the Secure Automated Firearms E-Check "SAFE" sysytem is planned to be available by Semptember 2023 for Snohomish County FFL Dealers. We are an approved participant in the pilot program, WSP anticipates rolling out the program in late June 2023. Weekends & certain holidays apply to the waiting periods and depend on the type of background check needed. Please reference the WA Legislature 9.41.090 and the WA DOL for the latest statutes and updates on waiting periods and background check systems.
One of Washington's newest bills HB 1143 is planned to be effective Jan 1, 2024, assuming availability of budget funding, concerning requirements for the purchase or transfer of firearms which includes a number of measures such as a pre-purchase license and additional training topics to existing training programs for all firearms i.e. such courses are available at Sporting Systems or NW Safe . Beyond required training see "Resources" section on page for additional more education and competitive shooting material.



Transfers

Interested in shipping a firearm to us, or need to pick up an online order placed through us?

Walk-in's are welcome, however we want to respect your time amd can't guarantee immediate availability, we might be busy working on other projects.
Appointments are greatly encouraged for pickup based on either the next day for the anticipated delivery date, or at least a few hours, after delivery.
Shipping carriers generally deliver packages in timeframes according to the type of service choosen to send the item(s). Learn more about the largest carriers delivery time expectations in this article . send a quick note by email or phone that the item is being sent Appointments are available, in as soon as a few hours, according to the type of firearm, "Pistol" or "Long, Shotgun & Other Transfer" , also available under "Schedule Appointment" in the naviagtion menu at the top of this page.
Please provide that two hour buffer after delivery to let us log the firearm(s) into our books, consider also, if you don't already, maintaining your own personal firearms record . If the item isn't able to be released during the initial pickup, please either reschedule the intital appointment or book a follow up "Shop Appt." appointment after receiving a notice from us that the item is ready for pickup.

A few items we'll need at, or by, the appointment to move forward; 1) Forwarded, or paper, copy of your receipt indicating whether or not state and local taxes need to be assessed. See DOR firearms dealer firearms dealer tax topics for more details. 2) An active photo identification (ID) including Name, Residential Address, and DOB. See ATF faq article for more details.



Selling a Firearm

Selling a firearm (private party transfers); whether it's a pistol, long gun, shotgun, or other seralized item requires the item to be received by an FFL holder, and often depending on the type of firearm and carrier, shipped from an FFL holder, with some exceptions. A tool available to verify a receiving FFL holders license is ATF's FFLeZCheck.

Selling a firearm online involves a certain amount of risk. One risk is that items usually are shipped before knowing if the item will be able to be released, background checks aren't performed until the buyer is in person. If the buyer is unable to take possession as a result of a denied background check, and the FFL Holder is unable to release the firearm, the costs to ship the item back to the seller are then assumed by the buyer. It would be a less common approach, however it is possible to ask for confirmation from buyer about eligably to own a firearm, especially if there isn't observable evidence like online reviews. A few assurance could include; requesting proof of a concealed carry permit, DOL firearms records certification avilable at local law enforcement agencies (LEA), or a criminal history report with WSP. Essentially have reasonable expectations on the risks of transfer terms, and basic policies of the receiving FFL Holder. Laser Engraving

Need to mark a firearm or personalize a product?
